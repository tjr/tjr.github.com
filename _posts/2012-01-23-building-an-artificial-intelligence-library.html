Lacking ready access to a good research library, I routinely add artificial intelligence materials to my personal collection. There are numerous good resources on the web, but sometimes a printed book is what you’re after. And amazingly, you can often appropriate gently used copies of those books for extremely low prices.

For example, I was just looking up the book Thinking Between the Lines, currently out of print, with new copies (for as long as they are available) selling for $50 USD.

Used copies are presently available through Amazon third-party retailers starting at one cent.

I’m not sure how selling at such low prices could benefit the sellers, but they set their own price, and I just bought a copy ($4 USD for one in like-new condition). Thank you, Mahler Books of Austin, Texas!
